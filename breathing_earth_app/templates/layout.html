Explanation

Read the full story at GeodesyGina.

Land height is influenced by both natural and human induced causes. Some examples include seasonal changes due to annual precipitation, earthquakes and tectonic motion, glacial melt, and pumping of oil, gas and water. This application shows the seasonal and longer term changes in ground height over an 8 year time period using high precision GPS vertical position time series. In this application, the time series were adjusted so that the position estimate is zero on January 1, 2008. At each time step, you see the change in ground height for each GPS station (circles) since that date. A rolling median was estimated to reduce outliers.

The Pacific Northwest is influenced by both seasonal changes in precipitation and tectonics. Seasonal observations are observed as the periodic change in circle color from red (summer, higher land height) to blue (winter, lower land height). Tectonic motion becomes apparant toward the end of the movie and is imaged as uplift (red circles) near the coast.

Alaska is influenced by seasonal changes in precipitation, glacial melt, and subduction zone tectonics. Note that the seasonal changes (circles going from blue to red) is apparant at the beginning of the animation, but then the circles remain red and just change size. This is because there is a strong signal due to both tectonics and glacial isostatic rebound that is causing uplift.

California , similar to Alaska and the Pacific Northwest is influenced by seasonal precipitation, tectonics, and human-induced water, oil and gas pumping. Note the discrete change in circle color in southern CA near the Salton Sea just north of the Mexico boarder between 2010 and 2011. This is due to the April 4, 2010 El Mayor earthquake which caused uplift in this region. Further north, pumping of oil and water caused large amounts of subsidence (> 200 mm over 8 years, or > 25 mm/yr).
